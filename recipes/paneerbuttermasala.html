PANEER BUTTER MASALA

Paneer Butter Masala is a classic and indulgent North Indian dish that is loved for its rich, creamy texture and flavorful tomato-based gravy. It features cubes of paneer, which is Indian cottage cheese, cooked in a luscious sauce made with butter (or ghee), onions, tomatoes, and a blend of aromatic spices. The dish strikes a perfect balance between creamy and tangy flavors,with a hint of sweetness from the cashew paste and cream.

The preparation of Paneer Butter Masala involves sautéing onions, garlic, and ginger until aromatic, then simmering them with ripe tomatoes until they form a thick and smooth gravy. Cashew paste adds a creamy consistency and a subtle nutty flavor, while a combination of spices such as turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, coriander, and cumin infuses the dish with warmth and depth.

This dish is a popular choice in Indian restaurants and homes alike, often served as a main course with naan, roti, or steamed rice. Its comforting and satisfying taste makes it a favorite among vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike, earning it a well-deserved place as one of the most beloved dishes in Indian cuisine.

LET'S START WITH IT

INGREDIENTS:

  • 250 grams paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed.
  • 2 tablespoons butter or ghee (clarified butter).
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped.
  • large tomatoes, pureed.
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste.
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional, for spice).
  • 10-12 cashew nuts, soaked in water.
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream or fresh cream.
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves).
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder.
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder.
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder.
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder.
  • Salt, to taste.
  • Sugar, to taste (optional, to balance flavors).
  • Water, as needed.

INSTRUCTIONS/STEPS:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of butter or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Once hot, add the cubed paneer and lightly fry them until they turn golden brown on all sides. Remove the paneer cubes and set them aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the remaining tablespoon of butter or ghee. Once melted, add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies (if using). Sauté for another 2-3 minutes until the raw aroma of ginger-garlic disappears.
  4. Add the pureed tomatoes to the pan and cook until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from the sides.
  5. Meanwhile, in a blender, blend the soaked cashew nuts with a little water to make a smooth paste.
  6. Once the tomato mixture is cooked, add the cashew paste and mix well.
  7. Now add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt to taste. Stir well to combine all the spices with the tomato-cashew mixture.
  8. Pour in some water to adjust the consistency of the gravy according to your preference. Let the gravy simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend together.
  9. Add the fried paneer cubes to the gravy and mix gently, ensuring the paneer is well coated with the sauce.
  10. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle it over the paneer butter masala. Stir it in gently.
  11. Finally, add the heavy cream or fresh cream to the pan, stirring continuously. Let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
  12. Taste the paneer butter masala and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer a slightly sweet taste, you can add a pinch of sugar at this stage.
  13. Once done, turn off the heat and transfer the paneer butter masala to a serving dish.
  14. Garnish with a drizzle of cream and some finely chopped coriander leaves.
